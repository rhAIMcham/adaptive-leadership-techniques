Dimension Technical challenges Adaptive challenges
Problem definition Clear and easy to identify. Difficult to identify and often vague or contested.
Solution availability Predetermined and available through existing expertise, procedures and rules. Unknown; requires innovation, experimentation and new discovery.
Responsibility Solved by an authority or expert. The people with the problem own the problem and the solution; requires broad engagement across the organisation.
Type of change Requires changes in procedures or processes within the existing system. Requires changes in values, beliefs, roles, relationships and mindsets.
Time required Can often be resolved quickly or in a specified timeframe. Takes significant time and sustained effort for learning and implementation.
Resistance Generally low resistance as people are receptive to clear fixes. High resistance due to the discomfort of potential losses (identity, control etc.) and the need for significant change.
Examples A power outage (call an electrician) or a broken bone (go to an orthopaedic surgeon). Addressing recurring performance issues, navigating a major digital transformation or fostering a more inclusive organisational culture.
